Hello, I'm Glad You're Here

My name is [Your Name], and I am a Licensed Professional Counselor specializing in perinatal mental health, EMDR, and trauma therapy in Fenton, Michigan.

I became a therapist because I believe in the profound capacity people have to heal — even from experiences that have left them feeling broken, stuck, or like they will never find solid ground again. The name of this practice, River & Rebirth, reflects exactly that belief: rivers move. Water finds its way. And even after the hardest winters, there is always spring.

My Approach to Care

Therapy with me is collaborative and relational. Before anything else, I work to create a space where you feel genuinely safe — not just theoretically safe, but felt-in-your-body safe. Healing cannot happen in a room where you feel judged or rushed, and I take that seriously.

I draw on several evidence-based frameworks, woven together based on what you need:

  • EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) — for processing stuck memories and trauma responses
  • Trauma-informed care — honoring the way trauma lives in the body and nervous system
  • Attachment-based approaches — exploring how early relationships shape who we are today
  • Somatic-informed techniques — tuning in to what the body carries and knows
  • Cognitive approaches — gently challenging unhelpful beliefs about yourself and your world

I am not a blank-slate therapist. I am warm, direct when you need it, and I will occasionally laugh with you — because healing doesn't have to be grim. You deserve a therapist who is fully present, not just professionally distant.

Why Perinatal Mental Health?

The perinatal period — pregnancy, postpartum, pregnancy loss, infertility, and the transition into parenthood — is one of the most vulnerable and transformative seasons a person can experience. Yet it is also one where suffering is frequently minimized or dismissed.

I have specialized training in perinatal mental health because I deeply believe this season deserves skilled, specialized care. Whether you are struggling with postpartum depression or anxiety, grieving a loss, processing a difficult birth experience, or simply finding your footing as a new parent — I see you, and I am here.

Outside the Office

When I am not in session, you might find me exploring Michigan's lakes and forests, curled up with a good book, or spending time with the people (and animals) I love. I believe deeply in the restorative power of nature — something that shows up in my practice as much as it does in my life.
